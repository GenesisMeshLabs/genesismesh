# NBA Team Design-Partner Pilot Offer

Status: valid and in progress; official first-customer package
Target buyer: player-development, athlete-care, innovation, or performance leadership inside an NBA team

## Pilot Name

Aspayr Athlete Financial Twin - Rookie and Transition Cohort Pilot

## Buyer Problem

NBA teams invest heavily in player performance and wellbeing, but financial resilience is often handled through periodic education, external advisors, or informal support.

That creates gaps:

- players receive information but not a living model of their own situation;
- rookies face sudden income, family, tax, relocation, and lifestyle decisions;
- traded players must reset quickly across city, housing, tax, and support systems;
- teams want to help without becoming financial surveillors;
- education impact is hard to measure;
- poor financial decisions can affect stress, trust, focus, and long-term player outcomes.

## Pilot Promise

In 90 days, Aspayr will help one opt-in cohort understand their financial trajectory through private digital twins while giving the team a privacy-safe operator view of cohort support needs.

## Pilot Scope

### Participants

- 5-15 opt-in players, ideally rookies, newly traded players, two-way players, or development-program participants.
- 1 team sponsor from player development or athlete care.
- Optional: team-approved CPA, player financial educator, or external advisor for review boundaries.

### Player Twin

Each participating player receives a private model covering:

- contract-year income schedule;
- guaranteed vs non-guaranteed income assumptions;
- estimated taxes by jurisdiction;
- agent, manager, legal, CPA, and advisor fees;
- housing, transport, family support, lifestyle, debt, and savings assumptions;
- endorsement and off-court income scenarios;
- injury, trade, no-second-contract, market downturn, and retirement runway scenarios;
- practical prompts for professional-advisor conversations.

### Team Operator View

The team receives only:

- participation and module completion;
- aggregate confidence/resilience score movement;
- anonymized common risk themes;
- cohort-level education gaps;
- requested support categories;
- consented case studies if a player opts in.

The team does not receive bank transactions, personal spending details, account balances, family payments, debt details, tax filings, or individual risk flags unless the player explicitly consents.

## Deliverables

1. Synthetic-data demo before any real onboarding.
2. Consent and privacy brief for players.
3. Player-private twin onboarding flow.
4. Team aggregate dashboard mock or pilot dashboard.
5. 90-day outcome report.
6. Optional public quote/case study only if mutually approved.

## Success Metrics

- At least 70% of invited players complete onboarding.
- At least 70% of onboarded players complete one scenario review.
- At least 50% report improved clarity on contract-year cashflow.
- At least 50% identify one advisor question they were not previously prepared to ask.
- Team sponsor confirms the aggregate view helped identify support priorities without exposing personal details.

## Commercial Shape

Design-partner pilot options:

- Paid pilot: fixed 90-day fee for one cohort.
- Sponsored pilot: no fee only if team agrees to structured feedback, named reference subject to approval, and follow-on commercial discussion.
- Expansion: annual player-care subscription per cohort or roster size.

Default recommendation: do not lead with a free pilot. Use a modest paid pilot or a highly structured design-partner exchange so the team allocates real attention.

## Discovery Call Ask

Ask for a 30-minute call with player development or athlete care:

> We are building a player-private financial twin for high-complexity athletes. We would like to test whether one rookie or transition cohort can use it to understand contract-year cashflow, tax, family support, lifestyle, and long-term security without exposing personal financial details to the team. Could we show a synthetic-data demo and pressure-test whether this fits your player-care model?

## Out of Scope For First Pilot

- regulated investment advice;
- direct money movement;
- team surveillance of individual finances;
- replacing agents, CPAs, attorneys, or financial advisors;
- using real player data in demos without written permission;
- any claim of NBA, NBPA, or team endorsement.
